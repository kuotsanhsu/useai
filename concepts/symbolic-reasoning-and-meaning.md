# Symbolic Reasoning And Meaning

This note captures a thread about why formal rules can reveal knowledge even when they look like mechanical substitution.

## Logic And Cognitive Significance

Rules do not manufacture new objects. They change access to consequences.

In a Fregean frame, a derived fact can be new in sense even when it concerns the same references. The value is not that logic creates a new world. The value is that a hidden consequence becomes a recognized judgment.

For code reasoning, this matters because a derived predicate such as `possible_cause(change, failure)` is not merely a label. Its meaning comes from its inferential role: what evidence licenses it, what rules derive it, and what intervention would challenge it.

## Proof As A Route

The railway-line analogy from the conversation is useful: a proof does not create the destination, but it creates a route by which the destination becomes reachable to thought.

This helps explain why "obvious in hindsight" is not the same as "cognitively trivial." A system can contain a consequence without anyone having the route that makes it usable.

## Kant Route

The Kant thread was not a full reading plan, but it gave a useful entry point:

- Ask how a claim can be necessary, informative, and not merely empirical.
- Treat `7 + 5 = 12` as Kant's pressure point for construction, not just lookup or verbal substitution.
- Read Kant through the problem of how the mind contributes structure to experience without making experience arbitrary.
- Keep Frege's later challenge in view: some things Kant placed in intuition may be reconstructible through logic.

## Meaning As Use

The later Wittgenstein thread matters for AI because "meaning is use" should not be flattened into pattern matching.

A better formulation: meaning lives in norm-governed use. A model can imitate patterns of use, but the hard question is whether it participates in practices of correction, justification, responsibility, and ownership.

That distinction is useful when evaluating AI-generated explanations. Fluency can resemble participation without carrying the same normative commitments.

## Psychoanalytic Symbolic Thread

The Lacanian thread treated the unconscious as structured like language: not a dream dictionary, but a system of signifiers, substitutions, omissions, repetitions, metaphor, and metonymy.

Matte Blanco adds another useful model:

- conscious thought uses asymmetrical logic: order, time, negation, causality, individuation;
- unconscious thought uses symmetrical logic: affective classes, reversibility, equivalence, part-whole substitution;
- ordinary psychic life is bi-logical, mixing both.

This suggests a possible neuro-symbolic psychoanalysis:

- neural models detect recurring language, affect, salience, and associations;
- symbolic models represent signifiers, classes, substitutions, negations, and temporal order;
- causal models ask which symbolic shifts are interventionally real.

This is a hypothesis to explore, not a settled method.
