# Attention Management Reference Map

This is cold reference material for `concepts/attention-management.md`. Load it when developing the concept, not during ordinary repo routing.

## Useful Map

- Philosophy of attention: William James, Christopher Mole, Wayne Wu, and Sebastian Watzl.
  Use this for attention as selection, action control, consciousness, and priority structure. See the [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on attention](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/attention/).

- Ethical and moral attention: Simone Weil and Iris Murdoch.
  Use this for attention as disciplined seeing and for studying how inquiry changes what can be noticed, while leaving value judgment to the user. See the [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on attention](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/attention/).

- Cognitive psychology: Daniel Kahneman, Anne Treisman, Michael Posner, Steven Petersen, and Donald Broadbent.
  Use this for attention as limited capacity, filtering, binding, alerting, orienting, and executive control. See [Kahneman on attention and effort](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6136270/) and [Posner and Petersen on the attention system](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev.ne.13.030190.000325).

- Forgetting as attention protection: Elbert Hubbard's retentive-memory line is useful as a cultural pointer, not as technical evidence. Use it to frame forgetting as disciplined release of what no longer deserves active attention. Pair it with psychology sources on inhibition and interference, such as [Hulbert and Anderson on inhibition in learning](https://memlab.bard.edu/files/2015/08/hulbert08_inhiblearning_brainbiomove_finalproof.pdf). The attribution is common, but should remain source-cautious until checked against a scan of the original collection.

- Economics of attention: Herbert Simon, Christopher Sims, George Loewenstein, Zachary Wojtowicz, and Josef Falkinger.
  Use this for attention as scarce resource, rational inattention, allocation under processing constraints, and a productive factor. See [Sims on rational inattention](https://collaborate.princeton.edu/en/publications/implications-of-rational-inattention/), [Loewenstein and Wojtowicz on the economics of attention](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jel.20241665), and [Falkinger on limited attention](https://www.iza.org/publications/dp/1538).

- Attention economy and platforms: Michael Goldhaber, Georg Franck, Thomas Davenport, John Beck, David Evans, Andrea Prat, and Tommaso Valletti.
  Use this for attention as market object: media, ads, platforms, attention brokers, celebrity, brand, and market power. See [Goldhaber on the attention economy and the net](https://firstmonday.org/ojs/index.php/fm/article/view/519), [van Krieken on Franck](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1440783318812111), [Evans on attention markets](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3044858), and [Prat and Valletti on attention oligopoly](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mic.20200134).
